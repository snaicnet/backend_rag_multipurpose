## Canonical Description

SNAIC is a strategic collaboration between SIT and NVIDIA. It helps companies adopt AI through applied research, AI solution development, and talent development. It also gives SIT students and professionals hands-on experience with real-world AI projects.

## Training and Talent Programmes

### Programme: IMDA 6-Month AI Training Programme

**Goal:** Train more than 200 fresh graduates and mid-career professionals over 3 years to become AI practitioners.

**Structure:** 1. 2 months of intensive training in GenAI, LLMs, and Agentic AI. 2. 4 months of work on real-life projects with industry partners.

**Outcome:** Participants gain current AI skills, practical project experience, and exposure to industry use cases.



### Programme: Applied AI Doctoral Training Centre

**Goal:** Train 10 industrial doctorate students annually on complex, industry-driven challenges.

**Project type:** Multi-year research projects that cannot be solved with off-the-shelf tools.

**Role in talent pathway:** Creates a pathway from AI practitioner to applied AI researcher and innovator.



### Talent Pathway Summary

The IMDA 6-Month AI Training Programme and the Applied AI Doctoral Training Centre together form a tiered AI talent development pathway.

## Collaboration Process with SNAIC

### Process Overview

The collaboration process has 6 stages: 1. Pre-engagement questionnaire 2. 1-to-1 consultation 3. Project scoping 4. Resource identification 5. Project development 6. Project deployment or scaling



### Step 1: Pre-Engagement Questionnaire

**Step name:** Pre-Engagement Questionnaire

**Purpose:** Understand the company’s needs and objectives.

**What the company does:** Fill in a questionnaire.

**What SNAIC does with the information:** - Reviews the problem statement - Matches the problem statement to SNAIC capabilities - Assigns the right Technical Lead for the first consultation

**Output of this step:** Initial understanding of needs and assigned Technical Lead.



### Step 2: 1-to-1 Consultation

**Step name:** 1-to-1 Consultation

**Purpose:** Explore whether AI can address the company’s problem statements.

**What happens in this step:** - SNAIC and the company have an in-depth discussion - They identify where AI solutions can create significant value - They clarify possible use cases and expectations

**Output of this step:** Initial alignment on business problem, AI opportunities, and expected value.



### Step 3: Project Scoping

**Step name:** Project Scoping

**Purpose:** Define the roadmap for integrating AI into operations.

**What happens in this step:** - SNAIC AI experts work with the company - Project milestones are defined - Deliverables are defined - The AI adoption roadmap is outlined

**Output of this step:** Project roadmap, milestones, and deliverables.



### Step 4: Resource Identification

**Step name:** Resource Identification

**Purpose:** Determine the resources, expertise, budget, and funding mechanism needed.

**What happens in this step:** - SNAIC and the company identify required talent, technology, and budget - Both parties align on a suitable funding mechanism - Both parties confirm resource contributions

**Possible funding mechanisms:** - Company-funded project - Joint grant application by company and SIT - Industry Doctorate or Industry Master’s project - IMDA 6-Month AI Training Programme - Integrative Team Project (ITP)

**SNAIC resource contribution may include:** - AI talent from SIT and NVIDIA - Postgraduate students - Undergraduate students - Key AI technologies - NVIDIA DGX H200 compute resources

**Industry partner contribution may include:** - Domain experts - Problem statements - Available data - Funding

**Output of this step:** Agreed resources, funding route, and role allocation.



### Step 5: Project Development

**Step name:** Project Development

**Purpose:** Build and refine an AI solution.

**What happens in this step:** - SNAIC works closely with the company team - A minimum viable project (MVP) is developed - AI models are selected - AI models are customised and refined - The solution is tailored to the company’s needs and objectives

**Output of this step:** Working MVP and refined AI solution design.



### Step 6: Project Deployment or Scaling

**Step name:** Project Deployment or Scaling

**Purpose:** Move the MVP into production or expand it.

**What happens in this step:** - SNAIC advances the MVP into a production-ready solution - The solution is deployed or scaled - SNAIC provides continuous support

**Output of this step:** Production-ready solution or scaled deployment with ongoing support.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### FAQ 1: Can a participant work and study during the IMDA AI Programme?

The communicated position is that participants may join if they take no-pay leave for 6 months. This avoids overlap between full-time salary, the IMDA incentive, and normal company work during training.



### FAQ 2: Can a participant use SkillsFuture or other government subsidies for the IMDA AI Programme?

No. The programme is already funded by IMDA. Other government subsidies should not be used because that would amount to double dipping.
